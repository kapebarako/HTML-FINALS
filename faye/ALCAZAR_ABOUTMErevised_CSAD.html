* Childhood...

My life had started when I was born at precisely 2:12 AM on January 7, 2001.1 I was born into a strict household, with my grandmother taking care of me and my big sister while my parents were gone for work.2 My prominent childhood memory was my grandmother teaching me much about discipline and so I must thank her for my strong foundation of self-control and responsibility.3

Aside from my strict household, most of my time as a kid was spent watching cartoons and anime.4 These fictional shows are what urged me to learn how to draw and I think, no matter how many times I’ve regretted drawing instead of studying, I can truly say that I’m glad I learned how to draw.5 Drawing has been my outlet to express myself better especially in a household where rules were prioritized more than emotion.6 Drawing has humanized me and has helped me cope with most parts of my life.7 In a sense, fantasy has been my means of escape.8

It was due to my detachment from reality, however, that I felt different from the rest of the pack.9 In my prep years, I didn’t have friends.10 I didn’t know how to interact with anyone and it was just awkward overall.11 It was like I didn’t belong and sometimes I think maybe that was why I was bullied.12 Another prominent memory from my childhood was all the bullying I experienced.13 Unfortunately, childhood was not the only phase I've experienced bullying but I shall focus on my first experience with being bullied in this section.14 At first, I didn't understand why.15 I remember that feeling of culture shock.16 I thought I'd have a group of friends with whom I share my interests with like those plotlines I see in cartoons.17 At that moment I realized that making friends is not that easy.18

My experience with bullying was most definitely not pleasant.19 The indifference I felt marked something in my brain that I must always do my best to fit in.20 However, it didn't work that well after prep.21 Grade school was not that different-- the bullying did subside but I still felt the indifference and in turn, I was hostile to everyone else.22 It was at that time as well that I thought maybe it's better to just retreat back to my safe space-- the fantasies I build inside my head.23

* Adolescence...

People often think about "teenage rebellion" when they remember their teens.1 Teenage years are the epitome of self-discovery.2 We find ourselves reigning on an identity that we best find ourselves in.3 In my case, unfortunately (once again), it was "teenage depression".4 I felt empty in my teenage years.5 I didn't know how I was supposed to talk, eat, act-- be.6 I had friends now who shared some of my interests and it made me happy but I still felt empty.7 It was the time that I think it was best for me to disappear.8 Retreating to my fantasies backfired as I lost hold of reality.9 My grades were dropping and my parents told me if this keeps up they'll have to remove me from school.10 At that point, I realized as well that I did not have dreams and aspirations in life and so I thought maybe it was time to have one.11

It was at the start of my high school years that I've decided to dedicate my time to studying.12 It was when I realized that when you're smart, people will talk to you.13 People would like to hear your thoughts and reason out with you because you are credible; you have the reputation of having good ideas.14 It was how I got to talk to more people-- they ask for my help and I deliver.16 I felt relevant for once in my life and it felt great.17 I was recognized by my teachers and even represented my school as they made me enter competitions.18 I was also better with handling my friends now as I gained more confidence to talk.19 I was good with science and so I thought maybe I wanted to be a chemist.20 Creating things has always been a worthy task that I'd love to dedicate myself to.21 The chemicals formed within and after a chemical reaction is what enticed me to this field of science.22 I saw chemistry as art and it gave me this connection between my dreams and my coping mechanism-- it gave me a reason to exist.23

* College...

It's a weird thought that I'm currently writing an HTML code for this website as well as the fact that I am enrolled in a Computer Science course.1 I can only say, life is weird.2 Applying for a college while having a financial problem is not a good combination.3 I was only given the choice between the University of the Philippines or the University of the East.4 Obviously, I failed UP and so I am here in UE thankfully with a scholarship that has enabled me to keep on studying.5 It was depressing, to say the least.6 I am ever grateful for a close friend of mine that made sure I was holding my ground and not a rope or a knife.7 I didn't handle the college rejection well, I thought I was capable enough to be in a school like UP.8

I had no choice but to move on.9 It was only a matter of circumstance why I ended up in this course.10 I heard they were offering a scholarship in this course and so I had no choice but to be in a course I had no prior knowledge of.11

My first year in college was unexpectedly great.12 I struggled a lot with keeping up since most of my classmates came into this course knowing what it would be like.13 I had to verse myself with programming knowledge and watched lots of tutorials online to help me catch up.14 Another thing that came to me as a surprise was when I joined the RnD unit which exposed me to the vast world of computer science.15 I did not expect myself to be active in the first year of a course I did not sign up for.16 Aside from the positive things, however, it was also the time when my mom got hospitalized and was diagnosed with stage 1 breast cancer.17 It was then that I urged myself to work harder in maintaining my scholarship.18

First-year was fun overall, I’d say I did find it hard to socialize with others especially with men having been in an all-girls school all my life.18 Second-year, however, still a work in progress but so far I’m not so sure if the online setup is ideal for me.19 It may be a tough year so far, with lots of mental breakdowns and existential crises , but I do believe I can work it out.20 I’ve gone through a lot and I know that I am still capable in a way and that one day I may finally learn how to love this course.21
